Observation 1

Consider the left endpoint \( a[i] \): if there exists an index \(i'\) less than \(i \) such that \( a[i'] > a[i]\) then clearly we have found a way to make the expression \( (a[i]+a[j])(j-i) \) larger. This leads to the observation that \(a[i] \) must be the maximum element in the range \([0,..,i] \). We can construct such an array, call it \(indices\_grow\), that stores the index of elements that are greater than all other elements to their left in time \(O(n)\). Similarly for the right endpoint, \(a[j] \) must be greater than any element to its right. We store the indices of such elements in an array \(indices\_decay \). Note that by construction these arrays fulfil the following properties:
  • \( indices\_decay[0] = n \)
  • \( indices\_decay[i] < indices\_decay[i-1] \)
  • \(indices\_grow[0] = 0 \)
  • \( indices\_grow[i-1] < indices\_grow[i] \)
Note further that both satisfy \( a[indices\_(..)[i]] < a[indices\_(..)[i+1]] \). Visually speaking you can represent \(indices\_grow \) as a positively sloped line touching the peaks from the left of the array and \(indices\_decay\) as a negatively sloped line touching the peaks towards the right of the array.
